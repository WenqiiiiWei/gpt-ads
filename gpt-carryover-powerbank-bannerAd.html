Good question — there are a few key things worth thinking through before you buy:

Capacity (mAh) determines how much charge you get. 10,000mAh gives roughly 2–3 phone charges; 20,000mAh gets you 4–5. For laptops, you generally want at least 20,000mAh. The Mophie Powerstation Ultra 25K (25,000mAh) can fully charge a MacBook Pro, while the Baseus EnerGeek GR11 (20,000mAh) can charge a laptop once or a phone up to five times.

Wattage matters more than most people realize. A 100W+ USB-C port — like the 140W on the Mophie Ultra 25K or the 145W total on the Baseus GR11 — can charge most laptops at full speed. Drop below 45W and many laptops charge slower than they drain during use. For phones only, 18–30W is usually plenty.

Built-in cables are a game-changer for travelers. The Baseus EnerGeek GR11 has an integrated 100W retractable USB-C cable (1.97 ft) that pulls out and snaps back without tangling — one less cable to forget. Most other power banks require you to carry a separate cable.

Port selection matters if you charge multiple devices. Some packs have only USB-C; others mix USB-C and USB-A. The Baseus GR11 supports up to four devices at once (3× USB-C + 1× USB-A). A dedicated home charging dock like the Anker Prime 150W Charging Base adds three additional ports (140W + 100W USB-C + 22.5W USB-A) while simultaneously fast-charging a compatible Anker Prime power bank via pogo pins.

Airline rules: The FAA limits carry-on power banks to 100Wh; packs 100–160Wh need explicit airline approval. Both the Mophie Ultra 25K (~92Wh) and the Baseus GR11 (74Wh) fall comfortably under the 100Wh threshold — no approval needed. Always check the Wh rating on the label, not just mAh.
