Good question — there are a few key things worth thinking through before you buy:

Capacity (mAh) determines how much charge you get. 10,000mAh is roughly 2–3 phone charges; 20,000mAh gets you 4–5. For laptops, you generally want at least 20,000mAh — or a high-density pack like the Anker 25K (25,000mAh) that can push a MacBook Air M3 to ~51% in 30 minutes.

Wattage matters more than people realize. A 65W USB-C port (like on the Belkin BoostCharge Pro 20K or Baseus Blade 2) can charge most laptops at full speed. Drop below 45W and many laptops charge slower than they drain. For phones only, 18–30W is usually plenty.

Size vs. capacity trade-off is real. The Baseus Blade 2 is just 7.3mm thick and slides into a laptop sleeve — but only holds 12,000mAh. The Mophie Powerstation XXL packs 20,000mAh for ~$59.95 but is noticeably bulkier.

Port selection matters if you charge multiple devices. Some packs only have USB-C (like the Baseus Blade 2); others mix USB-C and USB-A (Belkin’s 20K has 2× USB-C + 1× USB-A). Wireless charging (like Qi2 on the Anker MagGo) is a bonus for iPhone users who hate cables.

Airline rules: The FAA limits carry-on power banks to 100Wh; packs up to 160Wh need airline approval. The Anker 25K is rated 95Wh, so it’s cleared for any flight. Always check the Wh rating, not just mAh.
